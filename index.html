Assembling Brand Components

Fonts

Times New Roman

This is used for the first level of headings. Readability is often a concern that is tied to serif fonts, but as it is only used for the heading and is visibility larger than the rest of the elements, readability wouldn't be as much as a concern versus if it had been used for the paragraph elements. I decided to utilize New Times Roman Font, because it would add more of a professional perspective of the company and ensure it makes the parents feel like they have brought their children to a dependable tutoring center.

Verdana

For larger elements that are not a part of the header, I decided to use a sans-serif Font, Verdana. I thought Verdana would be appropriate because it contrasts well with the serif font used for the header. It does not take the attention away from the company's name, but additionally makes it more playful, allowing the page to be overall, more coordinated with the rest of the content from the page.

Helvetica

Helvetica is used for the elements that require the user to read. I decided to Helvetica after doing some reasearch, and I found out it is considered to be one of the most readable fonts. As a lot of my text is inquiring users of the services available at Auxiliary; I thought from a usability perspective, it would be more approporiate to use this typeface.

Potential imagery and/or art direction

In this section, I struggled in developing an art direction to be used for this company. A lot of times, parents want to ensure that their kids are having fun at their young age while learning, but also, want someone who is able to discipline their kids when appropriate. For this project, I found a lot of fun in developing both of these ideas to enforce trust and reassurance to parents. For the Art Direction, I thought it would be fun to incorporate elements that are typically seen in Cereal Box Branding; From my perspective, a learning center concerns both the parent's and children's attitude, and so I thought it would be fun to implement elements where it caters towards more playful branding, but are just as dependable when it comes to adult consumership.
